Amen

October 2, 2017

The weather of Kolkata is always surprising. It was same a few years back on a mid January's morning. The sun was proudly announcing that winter had passed. I was enervated in my full sleeve shirt. I was a little late for the event so I hurriedly opened the gate. And there she was. I saw a teenage girl standing infront of me. In her blue saree she was looking way mature. Her jet black hair just touching her shoulder and through the thick glasses I saw two emerald eyes, shining like crystals. Her lips were half opened between a laughter and a word that she had just withheld from saying. Her perfect skin was glowing like gold. For a moment it seemed that the time slowed down. She took her right index finger and pushed some of her hair behind her ear. Standing there I could feel the blood flowing through my vains, I could feel the pulses in my fingers. That's when a cold wind blew, through her hair touching her cheeks it found home on my lips. With a little shiver down my spine I pronounced 'amen'. Quite expectedly she didn't notice my presence. She decided to go inside and when she turned back, I noticed her hair was longer than that I thought. Within a moment it disappeared with her into the darkness of the room. For a brief moment of time I stood there still, thinking. What was it? The cold wind blew again while the sun burned my skin. It slowly wispered in my ears the most sacred word known to the mankind 'Love'. Love, that is ancient than the sun above. Love, that is purer than the blood in my vain. And love, that I felt just there. Oh you evil fate, I thought, I was brought to my destiny at the most unexpected manner at the most unexpected time. I met my Aphrodite. My life stood still and the whole event started rewinding in my head in an infinite loop. With slow breathing and still limbs my heartbeat spoke again 'amen'.

The Summer's Rain

March 8, 2017

A hot humid afternoon of summer. Burning sun above the head keeping no sign of shadow on the ground. It took her a little effort to open the big rusty gate on her way. With a loud noise, it informed its existence to the world. A cold breeze blew past her ears as she walked in, probably whispering something. She shivered. Her trembling footsteps on dry leaves made am uneven rustle. She was scared and lonely. But she knew she had to do this alone. Trees blew again, her hair all messed up. Oh, the beauty of almighty's creation. Naked eyes, messy hair and chapped uneven lips; the untouched beauty of a woman.​

As a child, she used to imagine a day like this. She saw herself walking down the aisle dressed in all white, holding roses in her hand, blood-red roses. The decorated aisle was covered with flowers and around that was her family and friends, cheering, wishing her for a new life, she imagined. But look at the evil turn of destiny. Today there is no one beside her. She is walking down the aisle, dressed in white with flowers in her hand, but people are replaced by silent trees and flowers by dead abandoned leaves. This is the first time she brought any flower for him, a blood-soaked rose, red like blood, fresh like dew. A few more steps will take her there. Five years were long to forget someone but one single message can dig out long forgotten names from the graveyard of memories. After all the time, she was finally there, standing in front of him. One single drop of water rolled out of her eyes and then the flood came with the flowing memories. Her weak knees couldn't hold her standing straight. She fell on the ground.​

Red rose on the green grasses gave a contrasting look with an angel sitting beside. She covered her face with her palm, kneeling down on the ground the lady started crying; long held tears finally broke out. The clouds above joined with her and the city bathed in its first rain of that summer. With first few drops of water dust on the epitaph removed and a name revealed. Somewhere in the heaven, God smiled and with the tryst of rain and sunshine, he drew a rainbow in the sky.

The Glass of Water

January 29, 2017

A glass of water felt happy and safe. He felt secure and home staying in there. “ it is my destiny”, he thought until one day the scorching heat of summer changed his fate forever. From his home, he disappeared into the air as vapor. The pain of breaking bonds was unbearable, the change was irresistible. He tried his best to hold whatever little he had, but when the storm comes it washes everything and in a moment you find yourself in a new place away from known faces. He felt the same. ​

As if his world was destroyed. He made some new friends, the refugee of the same storm, bearer of the same pain. And then one day they all became part of a cloud. By then he was numb with the pain. He understood that change is the only constant. Time passed by, days turned into months. It was time for him to say goodbye to the cloud. He was rain now. The dry land awaits for his touch. He can turn a desert into a valley. Yes, that glass of water who once felt content being limited was now feeling the taste of being limitless. He never knew that even he can be a part of a bigger goal. As a rain, he became a part of the river. He gave her flow he gave her current. When human tried to block her, he destroyed them. But when they came to ask for help, he gifted them with flowers and fruits. He gifted them food and life. And then the life goes on. Whenever he becomes impure the burning sun takes his soul and burns him to purity. Changes are still painful but now he knows it's for the greater goal, it’s for the better future.

That glass of water, who once was content with the little he had, now dreams to become a part of the sea, to become ocean one day. With a single stroke, his dreams and destiny changed forever. He bears the scars of the bad times, but he bears the sign as well that even a glass of water can become ocean one day.​​

When He Left The City

December 18, 2016

18th December, 2016. My wrist watch says it’s 1:30 in the morning and I am sitting at Bengaluru airport, about a thousand mile away from home. I am just one flight away from a new life, just a few hours away from a new dawn. Tomorrow’s sun will either guide me to a beautiful day or its sharp rays will make me blind forever. But tonight, sitting here while watching the crowd moving, I can remember the people I left behind. Late in the night, when the city sleeps, I made my great escape.​

The midnight’s flight allowed me to avoid goodbyes from the loved ones I never wanted to leave, my home and my Kolkata. The city of joy, the city that’s known for its rich culture and food, that is my city, that is my home. The city that gave me my childhood mornings, my school bus memories, and my college days. My evening walks, and late night sleepovers. She saw me standing at the bus stop, waiting, she saw me crying in the middle of the road. She witnessed my madness, she witnessed my dreams. She gave me scars and she gave me lessons. But above all, she gave me love. And she knows that I am addicted to it even after everything she did to me. Her one kind word could have kept me forever in her arms. So, she never did that. Her goodbye pushed me to the edge and probably it’s for the better.​

So to end, to all my friends who are reading this now, I remember you. I remember the memories we made together. Now I close my eyes and I close my note. I let the whispering wind pass my words to my city, to my love. I will come back because forever is not a myth.

When We Meet Again

July 9, 2016

I believe we will meet again, probably 5 years from now. When we both are grown-ups, mature than ever. We have our own scars we got from life. We have our own stories to share, our own mask to wear and our own beautiful lies to avoid the unknowns. Probably we will be too scared to give everything a new chance, too happy with the plastic life. When we are used to with the pain inside and we are used to with the fake smile outside. We will ask about life, we will lie about it. And then we will again move on. Probably nothing will change but we will meet again.​

I believe we will meet again, probably in 15 years from now, on an unknown street, between unknown faces. Probably at the busiest pedestrian crossing in Tokyo, I’ll have a glimpse of you. Or at a small café in a small town near Italy, I will see you have your coffee wearing your thick glasses with a book in your hand. I’ll see you at Time Square during New Year’s Eve. Seeing your husband by your side I will probably be too broke to go and talk to you. But I’ll see your smile. We will shout together to New Year. Or probably when we are two corporate geeks, we will meet in a corporate meeting where the conversation will start with a good morning and will end with a thank you, the formal one. But I believe we will meet again.

We will meet again, probably 50 years from now. When our children are too busy in their lives hardly to spare any time for us. When our stories are too old fashioned to our grandchildren. When we are too bored with our daily life. The dreamless dawns and the sleepless nights. We will meet down the memory lane. The old messages and emails will make us smile. My praises will make you blush even then. That smile on the wrinkled face, that pink cheek, I know, will be more beautiful than a red rose after its bath of the first rain. We will live in memories. But I believe we will meet again.

Or probably even late. We will meet at the heaven’s gate, just like the first time. You will be busy in your work, with the people around you; and I will again fall for that beautiful smile. The wild breeze will ruffle up your hair making it all messy, making you even more beautiful. The dark eyeliner will make your eyes even deeper to create a dark aura around you; just the darkness you like. And in that darkness, you are the brightest star. Probably that day I’ll have the courage to tell you about my feelings. Probably in the silence you will hear my fast heartbeats beating like a rhythmic drum. You will smile and I will smile and our eyes will meet promising a better future. I don’t know everything will be this dreamy or not, but I do know that we will meet again, in a new world, in a new universe, in a beautiful place. No matter what may happen, we will meet again.​
